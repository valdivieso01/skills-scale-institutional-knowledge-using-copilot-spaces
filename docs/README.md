# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation hub. This README serves as the centralized entry point for all project management process documentation used by OctoAcme delivery teams.

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management methodology is designed to provide clarity, structure, and repeatability for cross-functional delivery teams. The process begins with project initiation, where a Project Manager (PM) and Product Manager (PdM) collaborate to define the problem statement, measurable goals, primary stakeholders, and initial risks. Key artifacts such as a Project One-pager, stakeholder lists, and communication plans are produced to align all involved parties and provide a foundation for successful planning. Entry to the planning phase is gated by clear success metrics, stakeholder agreement on priorities, and team availability, ensuring that only well-prepared initiatives move forward.

During planning, OctoAcme breaks projects into shippable increments, prioritizes backlog items with acceptance criteria, and identifies dependencies and risks. The planning activities involve collaborative kickoff meetings, backlog estimation, and milestones mapping. Each backlog item receives detailed criteria and ownership, supporting iterative development and transparency. Risks and dependencies are logged in a register and reviewed weekly, with cross-team dependencies highlighted for escalation as needed. Roles—including PM, PdM, developers, QA, and stakeholders—are explicitly described, and responsibilities divided, promoting accountability and clarity throughout planning and execution.

Execution leverages daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and robust tracking via project boards. The team operates with well-defined workflow columns (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done), and enforces small, well-documented pull requests. Quality assurance is integral: automated tests, linting, security scans, and manual QA support reliable releases, while velocity and progress metrics are closely monitored. Blockers are escalated through three defined levels, beginning with team triage and progressing to sponsors for business-critical issues, ensuring swift resolution. Communication is facilitated by a cadence of weekly syncs, stakeholder updates, and ad hoc escalation, with templates supporting status, incident, and retrospective communication.

Continuous improvement is embedded through retrospectives after each milestone and incident, where learnings are distilled into actionable improvements. Action items are tracked with clear ownership and timelines, and their impact measured over subsequent cycles. The culture at OctoAcme emphasizes psychological safety, celebrating improvement and encouraging feedback as teams iterate on both product and process. This approach ensures the process docs remain living artifacts—centralized in the repository—providing all team members equal access to workflows, rationale, and knowledge, and supporting ongoing evolution in program management.
